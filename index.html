About the Project

The Autonomous Cars Project aims to expand the Queen Elizabeth II highway so that it will have extra lanes on each side. However, these lanes will be dedicated lanes for autonomous cars - cars that have self-driving capabilities.

Safety

How safe are self-driving cars? The technology is still maturing (it should be ready by 2020!), but even with our current technology, self-driving cars are incredibly safe!

Economics

Reduce Congestions

There already is a need for expansion for the highways between Edmonton and Calgary. However, with the addition of driverless cars, we believe we can reduce congestion even further, as they can drive in a much more precise manner.

Eliminate Accident Costs

Along with the addition of increasing safety, we can also decrease accident costs. From clean up to repair jobs to health related issues, self driving car lanes will be the first step to spending our money on something else more worthwhile.

Time Saving

Multitasking is not only difficult, but dangerous when driving. The dream of texting, watching movies, talking with people, and even sleeping during the three hour highway car ride can now come true!

Implementation

When

We're aiming to start construction as soon as 2017. There will be nine phases of construction and if all goes well, we should be done by 2025.

Where

Constructions will take place simultaneously going from Edmonton to Red Deer and Calgary to Red Deer.

High Occupancy Vehicle

We will be easing in the driverless lane to the highways. We will start with high occupancy vehicles like buses that can easily take advantage of these lanes.
